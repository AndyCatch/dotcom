Work Profile

The About part

I’m a digital product designer. I am currently employed as an Associate Creative Director, at R/GA. Up until the pandemic I worked out of the New York office (the “Airport”), whereas now I work out of the home studio that I share with my amazing girlfriend wife in Brooklyn. I have been at R/GA for seven years or so (more than half my time residing in New York). My day-to-day client is Verizon. In the past when the economic outlook was more rosy, there was more flexibility to move between clients, and I've worked on Goldman Sachs, Sonic Drive-In and Magic Leap. I grew up in Sydney, Australia.

I work with a team that handles systematic design, design guidance, internal facing products and brand compliance. At R/GA I'm placed in the Visual Design discipline, my role can swing into UX (including UX writing), design operations or straight UI. There will be the odd "blue sky" project, or "Vision" and they can be campaign or product focused.

My design journey up to this point is pretty bendy (aren't they all). I studied photography at University, but I had always liked tinkering with computers and gaming. I picked up a few freelance design gigs, and that started to pay the rent. Watching my fellow classmates suffer in their photo-assistant "work-experience" roles solidified that maybe being a photographer wasn't for me. I was curious enough to move into Flash Development, and was lucky enough to have a great mentor who taught me some basics of programming. When Flash died I was able to swing back into design (a credit to Flash's visual programming concept), but I had a different POV: knowing how things were built helped me solve visual design problems.

I certainly don't sell myself as a programmer, but I can code. I love to piece together parts of javascript to get a result. Tinkering can both increase knowledge and the time it takes to finish, but understanding HTML/CSS/JS and parts of React has helped me immensely in my design approach. I try to be methodical and systematic, and apply principles of "Object Oriented Design". If I had ample time I would spend more of it digging deeper into React, GLSL, three.js (or React-three-fiber!) and probably Unity or Unreal Engine. I get a pretty big dopamine hit learning new things!

How the site was made

I tried to make this site basically be the opposite of what I do day-to-day. The Verizon guidance is very rigid, so I wanted this thing to be weirder. I chose Beastly by OHNOTypeCo for some of the headlines, wanting to go for an absurd Western poster look, and I've always loved Klim Type, and so set the body and UI in Untitled Sans.

The site itself is built in Semplice / Wordpress. I basically use it as an engine for single page app transitions (it just works). To help iwith styling and interactions, I set up an local prototype environment using VSCode, Vanilla JS, SASS, Gulp and Netlify. Semplice is great, but the code editor is not. Should you choose the custom route in it, the workflow is difficult (but not insurmountable!). I'll most likely use it as an ongoing space to tinker and experiment.
